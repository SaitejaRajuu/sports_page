Sania Mirza:

Sania Mirza, born in Mumbai, India, emerged as a trailblazer in Indian tennis, breaking stereotypes and achieving remarkable success on the international stage. Known for her powerful groundstrokes and aggressive playing style, Mirza reached the pinnacle of women's doubles tennis, winning multiple Grand Slam titles, including Wimbledon and the US Open. She also attained the world No. 1 ranking in doubles, showcasing her prowess and consistency in the doubles format. Off the court, Sania Mirza has been a cultural icon and inspiration for aspiring athletes in India and beyond. Her achievements have not only raised the profile of Indian tennis but also encouraged young women to pursue their dreams in sports. Mirza's charisma, resilience, and dedication to the sport have earned her admiration worldwide, making her a role model for athletes and fans alike.

Roger Federer:

Roger Federer, from Switzerland, is widely regarded as one of the greatest tennis players of all time. Known for his fluid technique, versatility, and sportsmanship, Federer has amassed a record number of Grand Slam titles, including multiple Wimbledon and Australian Open victories. His effortless style of play and ability to adapt to different surfaces have earned him a global fan base and admiration from fellow athletes. Off the court, Federer is involved in philanthropy, supporting education and children's welfare through his foundation..
